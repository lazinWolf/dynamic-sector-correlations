# Dynamic Sector Correlations and Structural Compression in Financial Markets

Analysis of how financial sector relationships evolve across market regimes using rolling correlations, PCA, and structural compression metrics.

## Overview

This project explores how diversification weakens during market stress as sector correlations rise and markets become increasingly synchronized.

The notebook analyzes:

* Dynamic sector correlations
* Bull vs bear regime behavior
* Diversification breakdown
* Structural market compression
* PCA-based market synchronization
* Effective market dimensionality

## Techniques Used

* Rolling correlation analysis
* Correlation asymmetry analysis
* Principal Component Analysis (PCA)
* Rolling volatility & return statistics
* Eigenvalue-based compression metrics
* Time series visualization

## Key Insights

* Sector correlations increase sharply during downturns
* Diversification benefits shrink during crises
* Markets compress around dominant systemic factors
* Effective market dimensionality decreases during stress
* Downside correlations are stronger than upside correlations
